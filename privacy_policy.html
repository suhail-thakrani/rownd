1. What Rownd Is (and Is Not)

Rownd is a private committee (ROSCA / kameti / بیچی / کمیٹی) coordination tool for informal savings groups, especially in Pakistan.

We do not handle committee payments

Rownd does not process, transmit, hold, or custody money for your circle.

Rownd doesRownd does not
Record that a member declared they paid Move money between members
Let the organizer mark a contribution as verified Provide wallets, escrow, or bank accounts
Show cycle status, dues, and pool records Integrate with JazzCash, EasyPaisa, or bank rails
Store receipt images members choose to upload Collect card numbers, IBANs, or mobile-wallet credentials for committee funds

All committee contributions and payouts happen entirely off-platform—in cash or through payment apps and banks you and your members use directly. Rownd is the record and coordination layer only.

Rownd is not a bank, e-money institution, wallet, payment service provider, or escrow agent under Pakistani or other financial regulations.

  • We never receive committee funds into Rownd accounts.
  • We never initiate transfers on your behalf.
  • Words like “payment”, “paid”, or “verify” in the app mean status labels on records, not money movement through Rownd.

Organizer approval remains the trust gate for joining a circle; organizer verification means confirming a member’s declaration against proof they uploaded—not processing a transaction.

This distinction matters for privacy: we process coordination and record-keeping data, not financial transactions.

2. Organizer and Member Responsibilities

Rownd is a tool. Your circle is a private agreement between people who know and trust each other.

Rownd does not supervise your committee, guarantee payouts, collect dues on anyone’s behalf, or resolve financial disputes between organizers and members. Organizers and members are solely responsible for how they run their circle, move money, treat each other, and use the app.

By using Rownd, you acknowledge that all committee financial obligations are between organizer and members directly—not with Rownd.

2.3 Member responsibilities (رکن)

If you join a circle as a member (not the organizer), you agree to the following.

A. Joining and staying in the circle

  • Join only with a valid invite from your organizer or group.
  • The organizer runs the circle. Joining means you accept their rules, schedule, contribution amount, and verification process.
  • If you are not pre-approved, you may remain pending until the organizer accepts you.
  • Keep your display name and phone number accurate so the organizer can coordinate with you.
  • If you hold more than one slot/share, you are responsible for each share’s contributions and eligibility per circle rules.

B. Money — entirely off Rownd

  • Pay the organizer or fellow members using methods they specify—never through Rownd.
  • Pay by the due date your group agreed. Rownd may label you late in records if you do not declare or the organizer marks you late.
  • Do not expect Rownd to hold, release, or insure your contributions or pool payout.
  • When it is your pool turn, collect funds off-platform from the organizer or members as your group arranged.
  • Before joining, you are responsible for trusting the organizer and members. Rownd does not perform background checks or credit checks.

C. Declarations, receipts, and honesty

  • When you pay off-platform, use the app to declare that you paid for the correct cycle and amount.
  • Do not mark yourself paid if you have not paid.
  • If you upload proof (screenshot/photo), it must relate to your payment and be genuine.
  • Organizer-verified means they accepted your proof—not that Rownd confirmed the transaction.
  • If you declared incorrectly, coordinate with your organizer to fix records.

D. Discount committees — settlement review

  • When the organizer records a pool/settlement, you should review it promptly in the app.
  • You may acknowledge if correct or dispute with a note if you disagree.
  • Disputes are resolved within your group—not by Rownd support deciding who owes money.

E. Privacy and conduct

  • Other members do not see your full phone number by default.
  • Active members in the same circle can see contribution status labels for operational transparency.
  • Receipts you upload may be visible to the organizer and, per circle rules, other members.
  • Protect your PIN. Anyone with your unlocked phone may see your circle financial records.
  • If you stop participating, inform your organizer and settle off-platform obligations directly.

5. How We Use Information

We use personal information to:

  1. Provide the Service — accounts, circles, invites, contribution records, organizer verification queues, cycle reports, settlement notes, and reminders (not money movement).
  2. Enforce private access — row-level security, organizer/member permissions, subscription limits, and invite-only group visibility.
  3. Send notifications — in-app alerts and push messages about dues, verifications, approvals, and cycle events.
  4. Prepare WhatsApp-oriented dispatches — when organizers use reminder/share flows, we may format messages for delivery via configured provider webhooks.
  5. Maintain security — rate limiting, fraud/abuse detection, audit trails for circle dispute records.
  6. Improve reliability — crash analytics and operational monitoring.
  7. Comply with law — respond to lawful requests where applicable.

We do not use your data for unrelated advertising profiling.

7. Authentication & Device Security

7.1 Current sign-in model

Today, Rownd uses:

  • Your WhatsApp/mobile number as your account identifier
  • A 4-digit PIN for quick unlock on your device (stored locally as a cryptographic hash tied to your user ID)
  • Cloud authentication via Supabase Auth to sync your account across devices

We are working toward phone verification via OTP (SMS or WhatsApp authentication templates). When enabled, OTP will be used to prove phone ownership before account access.

7.2 What stays on your device

  • Your PIN hash and app unlock state are stored using secure local storage on your device.
  • We do not store your plaintext PIN in our database.

7.3 Session security

  • Access uses industry-standard tokens with refresh rotation managed by Supabase Auth.
  • Signing out invalidates your session on the device and server side where applicable.

8. Storage, Security, and Retention

8.1 Security measures

We implement safeguards aligned with our security baseline, including:

  • TLS 1.2+ encryption in transit (HTTPS only)
  • Managed encryption at rest for database and storage
  • Row Level Security (RLS) on operational tables
  • Server-side authorization for sensitive actions
  • Private receipt storage with type/size limits and group-scoped access policies
  • Rate limiting on sensitive public endpoints
  • Immutable audit events for security-relevant and dispute-relevant actions
  • No service-role secrets embedded in the mobile app

No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure; we work to reduce risk proportionate to the sensitivity of ROSCA coordination data.

8.2 International processing

Our service providers may process data in countries other than Pakistan (for example, United States or European Union data centers used by Supabase or Google). Where required, we rely on provider contractual commitments and standard safeguards for cross-border processing.

8.3 Retention

We retain information for as long as needed to:

  • Provide the Service to you and your circles
  • Maintain operational and dispute records for committees you participated in
  • Meet legal, security, and audit obligations

When you delete your account (see Section 9), we delete or anonymize personal data within a reasonable period, except where retention is required for ongoing circle dispute records involving other members, legal compliance, or backup cycles already scheduled for deletion.

9. Your Choices and Rights

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have rights to:

  • Access personal information we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate profile information in the app
  • Delete your account and associated personal data
  • Withdraw consent for optional features (location, push notifications)
  • Export a copy of your data

9.1 In-app controls

  • Notifications: Account → Notifications
  • Location: Account → Settings
  • Profile: Account → Profile
  • Sign out: ends your active session on the device

9.2 Account deletion and data requests

To request account deletion or data export, use Account → Privacy in the app, visit https://rownd.app/delete-account/, or contact privacy@rownd.app from the email or phone number associated with your account.

Account deletion requests enter a 30-day recovery window before final processing. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling a request. Deleting your account may not delete historical circle records that other members rely on; where possible, we will anonymize your identity in shared records rather than destroy the circle’s operational audit trail.

We aim to respond to verified requests within 30 days.

10. Children’s Privacy

Rownd is intended for users who can form or join legally recognized informal financial groups in their jurisdiction—typically adults (18+).

We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18. If you believe a child has provided us information, contact privacy@rownd.app and we will take appropriate steps to delete it.

11. Third-Party Links and WhatsApp

  • Invite links may open Rownd from WhatsApp, SMS, or other channels. Those channels have their own privacy policies.
  • Rownd does not control WhatsApp. Messages you send manually in WhatsApp are not imported into Rownd unless you explicitly use an in-app share or dispatch feature.
  • When organizers share invite text to WhatsApp, that sharing is initiated by the user/organizer, not automatic surveillance of chats.
  • Payment instructions shared on WhatsApp are organizer/member communications—not Rownd instructions to send money to Rownd.

12. Freemium, Trials, and Subscriptions

Committee money and Rownd subscription money are separate.

  • Committee contributions: never processed by Rownd (see Section 1).
  • Rownd app subscription (optional Plus/Pro plans): if you purchase through Google Play or the Apple App Store, that purchase is processed only by the store under its privacy policy. Rownd receives entitlement status (active plan, trial), not your full card number.

If you use paid plans or trials, we process:

  • Plan tier, trial campaign codes, usage counters (groups created/joined), and entitlement flags

16. Summary (Plain Language)

  • Rownd helps private kameti/committee circles track contribution records and cycles; it is not a bank and does not handle payments.
  • Organizers and members are responsible for all committee money, honesty, disputes, and off-platform transfers.
  • All committee money moves between members outside Rownd (cash, JazzCash, EasyPaisa, bank transfer, etc.).
  • Organizers approve members, verify declarations, close cycles, and record settlements—they do not act on behalf of Rownd.
  • Members pay off-platform, declare honestly, and upload genuine receipts.

  • We use Supabase and Firebase to run the app securely.
  • You can control notifications and location; contact us to delete or export your data.
